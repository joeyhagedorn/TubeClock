## Notes

*Parts flagged with a `*` are optional based on desired GPS configuration

Capacitors `C1`, `C5`, `C6` and `C8` must be rated for 16 to 25 volts! Other capacitors may be rated for 6.3 to 10 volts unless otherwise noted.

## Additional recommended items

* The [Adafruit Ultimate GPS Module](https://www.adafruit.com/product/746) may be used as an easy "plug and go" alternative to the `LIV3F` solder-on GPS module (and related components). This makes assembly quicker and easier for folks who do not wish to contend with the tiny 0402 parts necessary (only four are required!) to accommodate the GPS antenna front end required for the LIV3F module. **See the [TubeClock.GPS.md](TubeClock.GPS.md) file for more detail.**
* Any GPS module will require an antenna. Active antennas will yield the best performance (and might even work indoors!). Adafruit's [GPS antenna](https://www.adafruit.com/product/960) and the `Taoglas AA.162.301111` both work very well with either suggested module. A [u.FL to SMA adapter](https://www.adafruit.com/product/851) cable will be necessary to use either of these. If the LNA, SAW filter and related components are installed, it is possible to use a passive antenna; a DC blocker is recommended in this case.
* For infrared (IR) remote control, the [Adafruit Mini Remote Control](https://www.adafruit.com/product/389) is recommended. Other remotes may be used, however it may be necessary to modify the source code to accommodate them. `U4` might also need to be replaced if different modulation is used.
